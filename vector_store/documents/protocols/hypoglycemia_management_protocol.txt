Hypoglycemia Management Protocol

Purpose

This simulated protocol defines standardized procedures for recognizing, managing, documenting, and escalating hypoglycemia events within the MIRA clinical intelligence demonstration system. It supports Retrieval-Augmented Generation benchmarking and is not intended for clinical use.

Scope

This protocol applies to adult patients with diabetes mellitus, insulin therapy, glucose-lowering medications, recurrent hypoglycemia, hospitalization, emergency encounters, or documented low blood glucose values.

Recognition

Hypoglycemia may present with sweating, tremor, palpitations, hunger, dizziness, confusion, blurred vision, weakness, irritability, headache, or altered mental status.

Patients may also present with falls, seizures, loss of consciousness, or behavioral changes.

Assessment

Whenever hypoglycemia is suspected:

• Measure blood glucose immediately.

• Review recent medications.

• Review meal intake.

• Assess recent physical activity.

• Evaluate contributing illnesses.

Recent insulin administration and oral diabetes medications should also be reviewed.

Treatment

Patients who are awake and able to swallow should receive an appropriate source of fast-acting carbohydrate according to institutional policy.

Blood glucose should be reassessed after treatment to confirm recovery.

Patients unable to swallow or with severe neurological impairment require immediate escalation and emergency management.

Monitoring

Following recovery, patients should continue to receive blood glucose monitoring until stable.

The care team should identify contributing factors to reduce recurrence risk.

Medication Review

Medication reconciliation should determine whether insulin dosing errors, medication duplication, missed meals, impaired kidney function, or other factors contributed to the hypoglycemic episode.

Patients with repeated hypoglycemia should undergo additional medication review before discharge.

Patient Education

Education should include:

• Recognition of hypoglycemia symptoms

• Blood glucose monitoring

• Meal planning

• Medication adherence

• Carrying emergency glucose sources

• When to contact healthcare providers

Patients should understand how to prevent recurrent episodes.

Discharge Planning

Patients discharged following hypoglycemia should receive:

• Updated medication instructions

• Blood glucose monitoring plan

• Follow-up appointment

• Education documentation

• Emergency precautions

High-risk patients should receive early outpatient follow-up.

Escalation Criteria

Immediate escalation should occur for:

• Blood glucose remaining critically low

• Recurrent hypoglycemia

• Seizure activity

• Loss of consciousness

• Inability to swallow

• Significant neurological symptoms

Escalation actions may include emergency response activation, provider notification, medication review, and hospital admission.

Documentation Requirements

Clinical documentation should include presenting symptoms, blood glucose values, treatment provided, response to treatment, medication review, education, follow-up recommendations, and escalation actions.

Quality Metrics

Suggested quality indicators include:

• Time to glucose reassessment

• Documentation completeness

• Medication reconciliation completion

• Education completion

• Recurrent hypoglycemia rate
