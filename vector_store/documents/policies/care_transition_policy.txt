Care Transition Policy

Purpose

This simulated policy defines standardized expectations for safe transitions of care between healthcare settings within the MIRA demonstration platform. The policy supports Retrieval-Augmented Generation evaluation and should not be interpreted as real clinical guidance.

Scope

This policy applies whenever responsibility for patient care is transferred between providers, departments, healthcare facilities, rehabilitation centers, skilled nursing facilities, home health agencies, or outpatient providers.

Objectives

The primary objectives of care transition are:

• Reduce preventable readmissions

• Improve communication between providers

• Promote medication safety

• Ensure continuity of care

• Improve patient understanding

Effective communication should accompany every transfer of responsibility.

Communication Requirements

The transferring provider should communicate:

• Primary diagnosis

• Active medical problems

• Recent procedures

• Medication changes

• Pending laboratory results

• Required follow-up

• Outstanding diagnostic studies

Receiving providers should have access to sufficient information to continue safe patient care.

Medication Safety

Medication reconciliation should occur whenever patients move between care settings.

Medication discrepancies should be identified and resolved before transfer whenever possible.

Patients should receive updated medication instructions that clearly identify discontinued medications, newly prescribed medications, dosage changes, and monitoring requirements.

Patient Education

Patients and caregivers should receive education regarding:

• Diagnosis

• Medication changes

• Follow-up appointments

• Warning symptoms

• Emergency contact instructions

Education should be documented before discharge or transfer.

Follow-Up Coordination

Appropriate follow-up appointments should be arranged before discharge whenever possible.

Patients with complex medical conditions should receive coordinated follow-up involving primary care providers, specialists, and case management services when appropriate.

High-Risk Patients

Patients with heart failure, COPD, diabetes, chronic kidney disease, recurrent hospitalizations, multiple medications, or limited social support require enhanced transition planning.

Additional communication between providers should occur for these patients.

Escalation Criteria

Escalation is recommended when:

• Follow-up cannot be arranged

• Medication discrepancies remain unresolved

• Pending diagnostic studies require urgent review

• Patient lacks safe transportation

• Home support is unavailable

• Significant communication failures occur

Documentation Requirements

Documentation should include communication provided, receiving provider information, medication reconciliation status, patient education, follow-up appointments, pending studies, and identified risks.

Quality Metrics

Suggested quality indicators include:

• Readmission rate

• Transition documentation completion

• Medication reconciliation completion

• Follow-up attendance

• Communication completion rate
